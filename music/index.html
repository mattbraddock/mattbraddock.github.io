DIY Synth

Found below are the modules of my DIY modular analog synthesizer.
I've linked (as I can) to original schematics and included pictures of my work.

Power Supply

Converts 12VAC to +/- 12V and +/- 5V. Uses a 12VAC 1A wall wart supply which goes through a 1A panel mounted fuse and a SPST on-off switch.

Original Sources: 1, 2

Audio Mixer

4 mono inputs, each with level knobs, and 2 outputs (a 3.5mm headphones/auxiliary connector and a pair of 1/4" TS connectors), each with level knobs.

Original Sources: 1, 2, 3

VCO-555

Voltage controlled oscillator with CV, FM, PWM, and sync inputs, and sawtooth, sine, square, and triangle wave outputs.

Original Sources: 1, 2

Dual LFO

Simple dual low frequency oscillators with sine, square, and triangle wave outputs. Includes adjustable frequency, a low range switch, and rate indicator.

Original Sources: 1

Noise Generator

White noise based on a zener diode with filtered noise for other colors.

Original Sources: 1, 2

Dual VCA

Simple independent voltage controller amplifiers for control voltages and audio.

Original Sources: 1

ADSR

555-based envelope generator with gate in and manual trigger, plus an inverted out.

Original Sources: 1, 2
